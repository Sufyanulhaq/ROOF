Certi-Label Shingles

<& /comp/submit_review_widget.mas,brand_name => "Certi-Label", equip_type => "shingle", reviews_exist => 1 &>

Certi-Label™ is a label administered by the Cedar Shake and Shingle Bureau (CSSB) that qualifying cedar shingle manufacturers can apply to their shingles. The CSSB is a nonprofit trade association that specifies a series of standards for cedar shingles. Shingle manufacturers that are members of the CSSB, and whose shingles qualify for CSSB standards, can market their shingles with various Certi-Label trademarks. Shake products are marketed as Certi-Sawn, Certi-Split, and Certi-Ridge; standard shingles are Certigrade and Certi-Ridge; and pre-manufactured products fall under the lines of Certi-Cut, Certigrade and Certi-Groove.

Certi-Ridge shingle hip and ridge units are available in the same cuts as the Certigrade shingles. They are pre-manufactured caps for roof hips and ridges.

Customers may choose fire rating of "A," "B," or "C," with "A" being the highest, by asking for products with the Certi-Guard fire retardant. The company also offers products covered by a Certi-Last® treatment, which is designed to better preserve the shingles.

History

The Cedar Shake and Shingle Bureau (CSSB) recommends using Certi-Label shingles. CSSB is a non-profit organization that was formed in 1915 as a branch of the Trustees of the West Coast Lumber Manufacturers Association. In 1963, CSSB merged with the Handsplit Shake Bureau to become the Red Cedar Shingle and Handsplit Shake Bureau. The organization's current name dates to 1988.

CSSB functions to ensure that quality cedar products are used in all aspects of building construction. The bureau answers questions for the public, building code officials, architects and others. It also subcontracts mill quality control inspections to third-party quality control agencies.

Warranty

Through the association, approved manufacturers are able to offer with Certi-Label shingles a lifetime limited warranty. Customers can obtain detailed warranty information from the approved company. All treatment issues should be addressed by the company that provides the treatment.

As with all warranties, be sure you understand the terms before purchasing the product.
